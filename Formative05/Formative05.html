Purpose and Learning Focus

This formative assignment prepares you for the Summative05 Web Communications assignment by helping you understand how tools like Zoom are used in professional and organizational settings to support communication, collaboration, and coordination across distance.

You will explore core concepts related to online collaboration and reflect on:

  • how video conferencing tools enable real-time communication among geographically dispersed teams,
  • how features such as screen sharing, chat, polls, and breakout rooms affect communication quality and participation,
  • and how visible system output (screens, meetings, shared content) can later serve as evidence of completed work.

This is a low-stakes, preparatory assignment. You are expected to experiment, observe, and think critically-not master every feature or produce a polished deliverable. Grading emphasizes thoughtful reflection on communication effectiveness, not technical perfection.

After completing this assignment, you should be able to:

  • identify common ways organizations use Zoom and similar tools for meetings and collaboration,
  • explain how different communication features support or hinder effective interaction,
  • recognize factors that influence meeting quality, inclusion, and professionalism,
  • describe appropriate business uses for synchronous communication tools,
  • and prepare to document online communication activities for submission in the companion Summative assignment.

You are not expected to master every Zoom feature. Focus on understanding why and when professionals choose specific communication tools and settings.
Copilot or AI-assisted features may not be available in your Zoom environment; understanding the communication concepts is sufficient.

Exercise 1-Concepts

ref: MIS202 LinkedIn Learning

For this Exercise listen to the LinkedIn Learning course: 'Zoom Essential Training; ' and answer the following.

(10) 1. Why have tools like Zoom become standard in professional and academic environments, rather than just optional communication tools?
Focus on distributed work, cost, flexibility, and expectations in modern organizations.


(10) 2. How do meeting features such as screen sharing, chat, recording, and participant controls influence the quality and effectiveness of online collaboration?
Think from the perspective of a manager leading a remote team.


(10) 3. How can poor meeting practices (lack of structure, unclear roles, unmanaged microphones, or security issues) affect professionalism and trust in a business setting?
Consider both internal meetings and meetings with clients or external partners.


(10) 4. If an instructor, manager, or auditor could only see evidence from a Zoom session, what visible outcomes would demonstrate that the meeting was conducted professionally and effectively?
Do not list steps-describe observable results or artifacts.


Preparing for Summative05

(10) 5. How does understanding Zoom's collaboration, moderation, and documentation features prepare you to communicate effectively in professional web-based meetings later in this course and in your career?
Focus on readiness, not technical mastery.
